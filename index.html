        Game Manual

        This is the practical rules page: how each mode scores, how each control works, and how Fly audio starts.

        Speed Flags

        Identify the shown flag from four answers before the timer reaches zero. A wrong answer does not end the run, but it creates a large score penalty.

        • Correct score = base country value + combo bonus + fast-answer bonus.
        • Wrong score = at least -280 points or a bigger percentage hit from your current score.
        • Every streak raises the country difficulty pool, so the game becomes less familiar as you keep answering correctly.
        • The timer is the run limit. Your final score is saved into Runs and Leaderboard.

        Fly

        Fly the plane across the country map, manage fuel, and land in the target country or at a fuel depot. Distance to the landing center is the main score calculation.

        • Pre-flight starts parked. Click the engine meter, drag the brake lever down until it clicks, then the plane takes off facing east.
        • Once your engine is on you can move the mouse and this will increse your speed towards where you are pointing space to decelerate at constant 30m/s and or you can move muse closer to plane nose to decelerate fastr.
        • Takeoff requires speed, altitude, and balance. You cannot twist freely on the runway.
        • Manual steering works near the plane. Click the plane to release manual control; outside the control zone, autopilot holds a straight heading.
        • When speed reaches zero on the ground, you have landed and must run the takeoff loop again.
        • Fuel depots require a landing attempt: under 500 m/s and under 1000 m qualifies; 20 m/s or less is treated as ground speed and earns extra research points.
        • Target countries have no arrow. Use distance, the world map, and your route judgment.

        Fly Tech Tree

        Research points can be spent on three upgrade branches. Choose whether to fly longer for more opportunities or go faster for time-score pressure.

        FuelLower fuel burn, better route fuel return, stronger depot refuels.
        SpeedHigher thrust and top speed, eventually pushing toward sound-speed territory.
        HandlingBetter turn response, stronger banking, and cleaner recovery after sharp moves.
